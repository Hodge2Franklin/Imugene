Azer-cel (DLBCL) Program

Lead Asset: Azer-cel for Diffuse Large B-Cell Lymphoma

Clinical Results to Date

The Phase 1 trial of Azer-cel in relapsed/refractory DLBCL patients has shown promising results:

  • Complete Response Rate: 6 out of 8 patients (75%)
  • Partial Response Rate: 1 out of 8 patients (12.5%)
  • Overall Response Rate: 7 out of 8 patients (87.5%)
  • Safety Profile: Generally well-tolerated with manageable side effects

Next Steps

The Phase 1b expansion cohort is currently enrolling with results expected in Q3 2025. Planning for Phase 2/3 pivotal trials is underway, with potential initiation in early 2026.

Market Opportunity

DLBCL represents a significant market opportunity:

  • Annual Incidence: ~25,000 new cases in the US
  • Relapsed/Refractory Population: ~10,000 patients annually
  • Competitive Landscape: CAR-T therapies (Yescarta, Kymriah) priced at $373,000-$475,000 per treatment
  • Potential Peak Sales: $500M-$1B annually if approved

CF33/VAXINIA Programs

Oncolytic Virus Platform

Clinical Development Status

Imugene is advancing two variants of its CF33 oncolytic virus platform:

  • CF33/VAXINIA (Solid Tumors): Phase 1 trial ongoing with data expected in Q1 2026
  • CF33/VAXINIA (Bile Tract): Phase 1 trial initiated in Q1 2025, preliminary data expected in Q4 2025

Mechanism of Action

CF33 is a chimeric oncolytic virus designed to selectively infect and kill cancer cells while stimulating immune responses against the tumor. The virus has been engineered to express immune-stimulating proteins to enhance anti-tumor activity.

Competitive Advantages

  • Proprietary chimeric design with enhanced tumor selectivity
  • Potential for systemic administration (not just intratumoral)
  • Synergistic effects when combined with checkpoint inhibitors
  • Broad spectrum of activity across multiple tumor types

Regulatory Designations

FDA and Other Regulatory Designations

Current Designations

  • Azer-cel (DLBCL): FDA Fast Track Designation (granted Q4 2024)
  • CF33/VAXINIA (Bile Tract): FDA Orphan Drug Designation (granted Q1 2025)

Potential Future Designations

  • Azer-cel (DLBCL): Breakthrough Therapy Designation (pending Phase 1b results)
  • HER-Vaxx (Gastric Cancer): Fast Track Designation (application planned Q3 2025)

These regulatory designations provide several benefits including more frequent FDA interactions, rolling review of marketing applications, and potential for accelerated approval pathways.

Clinical Trial Risks and Opportunities

Key Risks

  • Patient Recruitment: Challenges in recruiting sufficient patients for multiple concurrent trials
  • Safety Concerns: Potential for unexpected adverse events in later-stage trials
  • Efficacy Dilution: Results in larger trials may not match early promising data
  • Regulatory Hurdles: Evolving regulatory landscape for novel therapeutic approaches
  • Manufacturing Complexity: Scale-up challenges for cell therapies and viral products

Key Opportunities

  • Accelerated Approval: Potential for expedited pathways based on strong early data
  • Expansion Indications: Successful platforms could be applied to additional cancer types
  • Partnership Interest: Positive clinical data could attract major pharmaceutical partners
  • Combination Strategies: Synergistic effects when combined with existing approved therapies
