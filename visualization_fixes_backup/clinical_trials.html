This chart shows the risk-adjusted Net Present Value (NPV) for each of Imugene's drug candidates under conservative, expected, and optimistic scenarios.

Azer-cel (DLBCL)

Lead Drug Candidate

Azer-cel is Imugene's most advanced and valuable drug candidate, targeting Diffuse Large B-Cell Lymphoma (DLBCL).

Key Information:

  • Current Stage: Phase 1b (completed with positive results)
  • Next Milestone: Phase 2/3 trial initiation (Q3 2025)
  • Expected Completion: Late 2027
  • Risk-Adjusted NPV: AUD 63M (expected case)
  • Peak Sales Potential: AUD 1.2B annually (if approved)

Recent Results:

Phase 1b results showed a 60% improved efficacy when combined with standard-of-care treatments, with manageable safety profile. The data suggests potential for breakthrough therapy designation.

HER-Vaxx (Gastric Cancer)

B-Cell Immunotherapy

HER-Vaxx is a B-cell immunotherapy targeting HER2/neu receptors in gastric cancer.

Key Information:

  • Current Stage: Phase 2 completed
  • Next Milestone: Partnership discussions for Phase 3
  • Risk-Adjusted NPV: AUD 33M (expected case)
  • Peak Sales Potential: AUD 500M annually (if approved)

Recent Results:

Phase 2 results showed a median Overall Survival (OS) of 14.2 months vs. 8.8 months for standard of care, representing a 61% improvement. The data has attracted interest from potential partners for Phase 3 development.

CF33/VAXINIA Programs

Oncolytic Virus Platform

CF33/VAXINIA is Imugene's oncolytic virus platform with multiple programs targeting different cancer types.

Bile Tract Program:

  • Current Stage: Phase 1 (ongoing)
  • Next Milestone: Interim data (Q4 2025)
  • Risk-Adjusted NPV: AUD 11M (expected case)

Solid Tumors Program:

  • Current Stage: Phase 1 (ongoing)
  • Next Milestone: Completion of dose escalation (Q2 2026)
  • Risk-Adjusted NPV: AUD 11M (expected case)

Recent Results:

Preliminary Phase 1 data has shown tumor shrinkage in 3 out of 10 evaluable patients with advanced solid tumors who had failed prior therapies. The safety profile remains favorable with manageable side effects.

PD1-Vaxx (NSCLC)

Checkpoint Immunotherapy

PD1-Vaxx is a B-cell immunotherapy targeting PD-1 in Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC).

Key Information:

  • Current Stage: Phase 1 (ongoing)
  • Next Milestone: Phase 1b expansion cohort data (Q1 2026)
  • Risk-Adjusted NPV: AUD 8M (expected case)
  • Peak Sales Potential: AUD 300M annually (if approved)

Recent Results:

Phase 1 data has shown antibody responses in 85% of patients with evidence of T-cell activation. Two patients have shown partial responses, and four have shown stable disease.

NeoPOLEM (MSI-high CRC)

Combination Therapy

NeoPOLEM is a combination therapy targeting Microsatellite Instability-High Colorectal Cancer (MSI-high CRC).

Key Information:

  • Current Stage: Phase 2 (ongoing)
  • Next Milestone: Interim data (Q3 2025)
  • Risk-Adjusted NPV: AUD 19M (expected case)
  • Peak Sales Potential: AUD 450M annually (if approved)

Recent Results:

Early Phase 2 data has shown promising activity with a 40% objective response rate in the first 15 patients. The trial is continuing to enroll patients with final data expected in 2026.
